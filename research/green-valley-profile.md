### 1.4 Veterans Population

**Confirmed: 22% of the 18+ civilian population are veterans** (~3,952 total veterans -- 3,724 male, 228 female). This is nearly 3x the national average (7.5%) and more than double Arizona's average (9.2%).

**Service era breakdown:** The community has a particularly large population of **Vietnam-era veterans** -- 5.92 times greater than any other conflict category. This demographic detail is significant for messaging (Agent Orange/VA healthcare, Vietnam Veterans Memorial recognition, etc.).

Many military retirees choose Green Valley for its:
- Proximity to Davis-Monthan Air Force Base (Tucson, ~35 miles north)
- Proximity to Fort Huachuca (~60 miles east)
- Low cost of living relative to other Arizona retirement communities
- Access to VA Southern Arizona Health Care System (Tucson)
- Green Valley VA Clinic (outpatient services available locally)

**Campaign Relevance:** Veterans are a major constituency -- nearly 1 in 4 adults. Issues around VA healthcare access, military pensions, and patriotic messaging resonate strongly. The Vietnam-era veteran dominance means Agent Orange exposure, PTSD recognition, and VA benefits modernization are particularly salient.

## 3. GOVERNANCE (CRITICAL)

### 3.1 Unincorporated Status -- What It Means

Green Valley has **no city government**. It is an unincorporated census-designated place, meaning:
- **No mayor, no city council, no city manager**
- **No municipal police department** (law enforcement by Pima County Sheriff)
- **No city zoning board** (Pima County handles land use/zoning)
- **No city-level taxation** (residents pay county property taxes, not city taxes)
- **No municipal services** (no city parks department, no city utilities, no city roads department)

All government services come from **Pima County**, supplemented by **special districts** and **private community organizations** that fill the gaps.

### 3.2 The Green Valley Council (GVC)

**Website:** https://gvcouncil.org
**Founded:** 1973 (celebrated 50th anniversary in 2023)
**Type:** Voluntary community association (501(c)(4) nonprofit)

The Green Valley Council is the closest thing Green Valley has to a local government, but it has **no taxing authority, no police power, and no regulatory jurisdiction**. It functions as:

- **Community voice:** Represents Green Valley's interests to Pima County government
- **Coordination body:** Coordinates among the community's many HOAs
- **Civic engagement hub:** Organizes community forums, candidate events, informational meetings
- **Advocacy organization:** Lobbies county/state/federal officials on behalf of GV residents

**Structure:**
- Member HOAs pay dues to belong to the GVC
- The GVC has a board of directors elected from member HOA representatives
- Standing committees (health & wellness, community affairs, etc.)
- The GVC Foundation (separate 501(c)(3)) handles charitable activities

**Key Navigation Links Found:**
- HOA Portal (directory of member HOAs)
- Boards & Committees
- Community Partners
- Events Calendar
- The Foundation (gvc-foundation.org)

**Campaign Relevance:** The GVC is the gateway to organized civic life in Green Valley. Any candidate must present to and be endorsed by (or at least neutral with) the GVC. Their community forums and events are the primary venue for political engagement.

### 3.3 The HOA Structure

Wikipedia notes Green Valley is composed of **59 Homeowner Associations** (the research plan cites 100+, which likely includes sub-associations and smaller entities within larger master-planned communities).

Each HOA is an independent corporation with:
- Its own elected board of directors
- Its own CC&Rs (covenants, conditions, and restrictions)
- Its own dues structure
- Its own rules for property maintenance, architectural standards, etc.

The GVC has a dedicated "HOA Lister" plugin on its website, suggesting it maintains a directory of all member HOAs.

**Campaign Relevance:** HOA boards are microcosms of local politics. Board presidents are community influencers who can facilitate or block candidate access to their neighborhoods. Understanding the HOA governance structure is essential for canvassing strategy.

## 4. KEY ISSUES

### 4.1 Water Table Decline (CRITICAL)

**The single most important long-term issue facing Green Valley.**

Per the 2007 Pima County report (cited in Wikipedia with specific data):

- **76,000 acre-feet** of water pumped from the Upper Santa Cruz Valley aquifer in 2006
- **85% of that water** goes to **mining and agriculture** -- NOT residential use
- Only **15%** is split between golf courses and residential/commercial use
- The report concluded: *"The Green Valley area does not have a sustainable water supply given current groundwater pumping rates"*
- The report warned: *"Water supplies will become critical within the next ten years"* (i.e., by ~2017)

**Major Water Users (all pumping from the same aquifer):**
1. ASARCO Mission Mine (industrial-scale)
2. Freeport-McMoRan Sierrita Mine (industrial-scale)
3. Farmers Investment Co. / FICO (pecan farming, industrial-scale)
4. Golf courses (multiple in Green Valley)
5. Residential water providers (multiple small companies)

**Water Level Decline Rate (CONFIRMED):**
- Water companies serving the Sahuarita-Green Valley area report declines averaging **4.5 feet per year since 1999** and **5 feet per year since 2009**
- In some localized areas, decline reaches **2 feet per year**
- The aquifer suffers from: excessive groundwater pumping, a lack of recharge, AND a **sulfate plume spreading east from the mines** west of I-19

**Mitigation Efforts:**
- The 2007 report recommended bringing Central Arizona Project (CAP) renewable water to the Upper Santa Cruz Basin via a 72-inch pipeline
- As of 2017, one 36-inch CAP pipeline was completed, with a second 36-inch pipeline in progress
- **Critical math:** Two 36-inch pipes provide only **HALF** the capacity of a single 72-inch pipe (capacity scales with cross-sectional area, not diameter)
- **Project RENEWS** (Community Water Company): Currently constructing a pipeline and underground water storage facility to bring Colorado River water to Green Valley -- a direct response to the crisis
- USCPUG has published water use projections through 2030
- Partnership with U.S. Bureau of Reclamation for Colorado River water transport is underway but funding remains the major challenge

**Arizona Water Law Problem:** Arizona water law places **few limits on the amount of water that can be pumped** -- costs are limited only to drilling, pumping, and distribution. This means mining companies can pump virtually unlimited water with no legal constraint, even as the aquifer declines. Green Valley is NOT within an Active Management Area (AMA), meaning it lacks even the basic groundwater regulation that applies to Phoenix and Tucson.

**Campaign Messaging:** This is a powerful issue. Residents' drinking water is literally being drained by mining corporations that contribute to the tax base but threaten the community's long-term viability. A candidate could advocate for:
- Stricter groundwater pumping regulations for industrial users
- Accelerated CAP pipeline construction
- Water recycling mandates for mining operations
- State-level reform of groundwater law

### 4.2 Healthcare Access (CRITICAL -- HOSPITAL CLOSED)

**MAJOR DEVELOPMENT: Santa Cruz Valley Regional Hospital CLOSED June 30, 2022.**

This 49-bed acute care hospital was the **only hospital within 26 miles** of Green Valley. Its closure is one of the most significant quality-of-life crises facing the community.

**Closure Details:**
- Closed June 30, 2022 due to insurmountable debt
- The hospital had been paying back ~$400,000/month in Medicare pandemic advance payments
- TMC HealthCare (Tucson Medical Center) was poised to purchase the facility but **backed out** at the last moment
- The closure eliminated 300 jobs and ended 7 years of local hospital service
- The hospital's ER had been seeing ~50 patients/day
- The facility served roughly **100,000 people** along the I-19 corridor
- **Private equity ownership** collected millions in federal pandemic aid before shuttering -- a politically potent detail

**Reopening Status (as of early 2026):**
- Efforts to reopen have been ongoing since 2022 but **no firm timeline or commitment exists**
- Multiple offers have been received by the broker
- The hospital building at 4455 I-19 Frontage Road remains closed
- Green Valley Buzz reporting suggests reopening is unlikely in the near term

**Current Healthcare Available:**
- **United Community Health Center (UCHC):** Operates 5 primary care clinic sites in Green Valley (plus locations in Amado, Arivaca, Sahuarita, Three Points, Vail). Federally qualified community health center providing primary care -- NOT hospital/emergency services. UCHC at Green Valley Hospital offers Family Practice at 4475 I-19 Frontage Rd, Suite 139.
- **NextCare Urgent Care:** Opened November 2023 at The Crossing at Sahuarita -- urgent care only, not emergency services
- **For emergency/hospital care:** Residents must now drive **25-45 minutes to Tucson** (Banner-University Medical Center, St. Joseph's Hospital, TMC Healthcare)

**Healthcare Concerns:**
- **NO LOCAL EMERGENCY ROOM** -- the most acute concern for a community with a median age of 73.3. Every heart attack, stroke, or fall now requires a 45-minute ambulance ride to Tucson
- **Specialist access:** Limited specialist physicians in Green Valley; most specialty care requires Tucson trips
- **Emergency response times:** Santa Rita Fire District EMS responds locally, but transfer to trauma centers takes 30-45 minutes minimum
- **Medicare dependency:** 56.8% of the population is on Medicare and 97.6% have health coverage; any changes to Medicare coverage, premiums, or provider networks directly impact nearly every household
- **Prescription drug costs:** Major concern for fixed-income seniors on multiple medications
- **Long-term care:** Limited assisted living and memory care facilities relative to population
- **Mental health:** Social isolation among elderly, especially those living alone (28.5% of households); depression and cognitive decline concerns
- **VA healthcare:** Green Valley VA Clinic provides outpatient services; full VA hospital is in Tucson

**Campaign Relevance:** The hospital closure is arguably the single most emotionally resonant issue in Green Valley. "Our seniors lost their only hospital while a private equity firm walked away with millions in federal money" is a message that crosses party lines. Advocating for state-level action to prevent rural hospital closures, support community health infrastructure, and incentivize hospital operations in underserved areas is a powerful platform plank.

### 4.3 Property Taxes and Services Gap

Green Valley's effective property tax rate is **0.73%** -- below the national median (1.02%) but above Arizona's state median (0.56%). Residents pay **Pima County property taxes** but receive a more limited service package than incorporated cities because:
- No municipal services (parks, recreation, code enforcement are handled privately through HOAs and GVR)
- Sheriff patrols cover a large area with limited staffing compared to a city police department
- Road maintenance is county responsibility but lower priority than urban Tucson areas
- No municipal court for quality-of-life violations
- Residents pay into school district taxes despite only ~2% having school-age children

This creates a persistent perception that residents "pay for Tucson's services" while receiving less in return. It is a recurring complaint, amplified by the fact that most residents are on fixed incomes where even small property tax increases are felt acutely. With home values rising (up 6.5% YoY to a median of $293K), property tax assessments are climbing even as monthly income remains fixed.

### 4.4 Incorporation Debate

The question of whether Green Valley should incorporate as a city has come up on an almost cyclical basis but has never gained majority support.

**Legal Barrier (IMPORTANT):** A 1961 Arizona law gives neighboring towns the power to **veto** incorporations. This means any move to incorporate Green Valley would require the **blessing of the Town of Sahuarita**. In 1997, the state Legislature passed a law exempting Pima County from the 1961 law, but the **Arizona Supreme Court declared the 1997 law unconstitutional**. This legal barrier remains in place, effectively giving Sahuarita a veto over Green Valley's incorporation.

**For Incorporation:**
- Local control over zoning, development, and land use
- Dedicated police force (city police vs. county sheriff)
- Ability to set local priorities and budget
- More responsive government for local issues

**Against Incorporation:**
- Would require local taxes (city property tax, sales tax) on top of county taxes
- Residents chose Green Valley specifically to avoid city government overhead
- HOA governance already handles most neighborhood-level issues
- Most residents are on fixed incomes and resistant to any tax increases
- The GVC provides adequate advocacy without the cost of a city bureaucracy
- Green Valley's strong volunteer culture means many services are provided through community self-organization

**Current Status:** Incorporation is not actively being pursued. The legal barrier (Sahuarita's veto power) makes it even less likely. The community has generally preferred the status quo of HOA governance + GVC advocacy + county services. The culture is one of self-reliance: "a culture where people want to do for themselves and help their neighbors."

- **Green Valley Transportation Service:** Family-owned taxi/transport service, 24/7. (520) 548-5858.
- **Green Valley Airport Shuttle:** Serves TUS (Tucson International) airport.

**Key Issue:** Car dependency is the defining transportation challenge. Despite the elderly population, most residents drive; when they can no longer drive, mobility becomes a critical quality-of-life issue. With the hospital closure, the transportation burden has intensified -- non-emergency medical transport to Tucson for specialist appointments that used to be available locally is now a 45-minute each-way trip.

### 4.6 Medicare and Social Security

**Confirmed data points:**
- **83.7%** of households receive Social Security (highest rate in Southern Arizona)
- **68.1%** of households receive retirement income
- **56.8%** of the population is on Medicare
- Only **24.7%** of households have earned income
- The nearest Social Security office serves GV zip codes 85614 and 85622

Given these numbers:
- **Any proposed cuts to Social Security are existential threats** to this community -- it is not hyperbole to say that 5 out of 6 households would be directly affected
- **Medicare premium increases** directly impact household budgets
- **Part D prescription drug coverage** is a top concern
- **Social Security COLA adjustments** may not keep pace with actual cost-of-living increases in Arizona (housing up 6.5% YoY, healthcare costs, utilities)
- **SHIP (State Health Insurance Program)** through Pima Council on Aging (520-790-7262, PCOA.org) provides Medicare counseling -- a resource candidates should know about and reference

**Arizona State Retirement System (ASRS)** also affects former state/county/school employees who retired to Green Valley.

This is THE bread-and-butter issue for Green Valley voters and likely the most persuasive campaign message: *"I will fight to protect your Social Security and Medicare."*

### 4.7 HOA Governance Issues

Common complaints in HOA-governed communities apply:
- Board overreach/arbitrary rule enforcement
- Transparency of HOA finances
- Aging infrastructure in older subdivisions (roads, common areas, pools)
- Special assessment fees for major repairs
- HOA fee increases on fixed incomes
- Disputes between residents and boards
- CC&R enforcement inconsistencies

### 5.7 Titan Missile Museum

**Location:** Green Valley (Duval Mine Road)
**Type:** National Historic Landmark
**Significance:** The last of 54 Titan II missile sites that were on alert across the US from 1963-1987. Only preserved Titan II ICBM silo in the world. Operated by the Arizona Aerospace Foundation.

**Visitor Experience:** 45-minute guided underground tours start every hour on the hour (10am-4pm). Visitors see three-ton blast doors, eight-foot-thick silo walls, and an actual 104-foot Titan II missile standing "on alert" in the launch duct. A powerful Cold War heritage experience.

**Campaign Relevance:** Perfect venue for national security/veteran messaging events. The museum draws visitors from across the country and is a point of community pride, especially among the veteran population.

## 6. CAMPAIGN STRATEGY IMPLICATIONS

### 6.1 Voter Profile Summary

Green Valley voters are predominantly:
- White, elderly, well-educated retirees
- Military veterans and their spouses (significant minority)
- Fixed-income households dependent on Social Security/pensions
- Homeowners with high property equity but limited cash flow
- Politically engaged (high voter turnout is typical in retirement communities)
- Generally conservative but pragmatic on pocketbook issues
- Concerned about healthcare access, water security, and cost of living

### 6.2 Winning Messages for Green Valley

1. **Social Security and Medicare Protection** -- This is the #1 issue. 83.7% of households receive Social Security. "I will never vote to cut or privatize Social Security or Medicare."
2. **Hospital Restoration** -- NEW #2 ISSUE. "A private equity company took millions in federal pandemic money, then closed your only hospital. I'll fight for state action to restore hospital services to Green Valley."
3. **Water Security** -- "Mining companies are draining your aquifer at 5 feet per year while a sulfate plume spreads toward your homes. I will fight for fair water allocation and accelerated CAP pipeline funding."
4. **Property Tax Fairness** -- "You pay county taxes but get fewer services. Your home values are rising 6.5% a year, pushing up tax bills on fixed incomes. I'll advocate for senior property tax relief."
5. **Veteran Services** -- "One in four of your neighbors served this country. Our veterans deserve better than a 40-minute drive to the VA hospital."
6. **Cost of Living** -- "When poverty among Green Valley seniors has tripled in two decades, something is broken. I'll fight for prescription drug price reforms and utility rate protections."
7. **Senior Transportation** -- "When you can't drive yourself to a specialist 45 minutes away, you need someone fighting for better transit options."

### 6.3 Access Strategy

| Venue/Channel | Approach |
|---------------|----------|
| Green Valley Council | Request speaking slot at community forum; attend GVC events |
| GVR Recreation Centers | Hold town halls at GVR facilities (14 locations!) |
| HOA Board Meetings | Request time to address individual HOA boards |
| Green Valley News | Write op-eds, attend editorial board, earn coverage |
| Friends in Deed | Volunteer; demonstrate community commitment |
| Churches | Attend services; speak at church-affiliated community events |
| VFW/American Legion | Attend events; honor veteran service in messaging |
| Titan Missile Museum | Photo op / event venue for national security messaging |
| Sun Shuttle | Ride public transit to demonstrate understanding of senior mobility issues |

### 6.4 Canvassing Notes

- **Door-to-door viability:** Excellent. Subdivision layouts with clear streets, numbered homes, and walkable neighborhoods make canvassing efficient.
- **Best times:** Mid-morning (9-11am) or late afternoon (3-5pm). Avoid mid-day heat.
- **Seasonal consideration:** Many residents are **snowbirds** (seasonal, October-April). Canvass during peak season (January-March) for maximum contact.
- **Accessibility:** Be prepared for residents with mobility issues answering doors slowly; be patient and respectful.
- **Security concerns:** Many HOAs have gate access or neighborhood watch; coordinate with HOA boards for entry permission.
